About InternPhish

Last updated — May 2025

Why this exists

Every year, thousands of Indian students — freshers hungry for experience, anxious about their resume — receive WhatsApp messages or emails that look like job offers. They arrive instantly, without any application. They promise certificates, huge stipends, and remote work. They ask for a small "registration fee" to secure the slot.

These are scams. And they work because students are desperate, because the language feels professional, and because there's no easy place to check.

InternPhish was built to change that. It's a searchable, community-driven registry of reported fake internship offers. If an org name is here, someone was scammed by them. Search before you pay.

How it works

  • Anyone can submit a report — no login needed.
  • Our scam-pattern engine auto-tags the message with red-flag phrases.
  • Reports are public and searchable on the Reports Hub.
  • Similar reports are grouped so you can see patterns across organizations.
  • The Breakdown page educates users on what each scam phrase means and why it works.

What we are not

InternPhish is not a legal authority. We don't file FIRs or initiate cyber-crime cases (though we strongly encourage you to). We are not affiliated with any government body, NSDC, or job platform. We are a public awareness tool — we hold a mirror up.

If your organization has been reported here and you believe it's in error, contact the site owner with evidence and we will review.
